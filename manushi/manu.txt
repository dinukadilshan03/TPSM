CORRELATION ANALYSIS

Correlation analysis was conducted to examine the relationship between engagement metrics and content popularity (views).

Descriptive Correlation Analysis

Descriptive correlation was performed by computing the Pearson correlation coefficient using the correlation matrix. This was done using:

df.corr()

The Pearson correlation coefficient is defined as:

r=Cov(X,Y)/(σXσY)

Where:

Cov(X,Y) is the covariance between variables

σX and σY are the standard deviations of the variables

This step was used to measure the strength and direction of linear relationships between continuous variables such as views, likes, shares, comments, engagement_rate, and sentiment_score.

At this stage, the analysis was purely descriptive, as it only reported the correlation values without testing statistical significance.

Inferential Correlation Analysis

To determine whether the observed correlation was statistically significant at the population level, Pearson’s correlation test was performed using:

scipy.stats.pearsonr()

The following hypotheses were tested:

H0: There is no linear relationship between the variables (ρ = 0).
H1: There is a significant linear relationship between the variables (ρ ≠ 0).

The p-value obtained from the test was compared against a significance level of 0.05.

Decision Rule:
If p < 0.05 → Reject H0
If p ≥ 0.05 → Fail to reject H0

If the p-value was less than 0.05, the relationship was considered statistically significant.

In summary, descriptive correlation was used to explore relationship strength, while inferential correlation testing was used to determine whether the relationship was statistically significant.
